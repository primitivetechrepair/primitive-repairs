Microsoldering FAQs

Common questions before submitting a request.

What is microsoldering repair?

Microsoldering is board-level repair that uses magnification and precision soldering to work on small ports, pads, connectors, traces, and electronic components that cannot be repaired with basic part replacement alone.

What problems may require microsoldering?

Common issues include damaged charging ports, USB-C problems, HDMI port damage, no-power symptoms, broken connector pads, liquid damage, corrosion, and small board-level failures.

Can every board-level issue be repaired?

No. Some boards may be too damaged, too corroded, missing important pads or traces, or not practical to repair compared to replacement. Final options are confirmed after inspection.

Do you repair HDMI ports on game consoles?

Yes. Primitive Tech Repairs can review HDMI port repair requests for supported consoles, including damaged HDMI ports, broken solder joints, lifted pads, and display output issues.

Is final pricing shown before inspection?

Microsoldering pricing depends on the device model, board condition, damage severity, part availability, and whether the repair is practical. Submit a request with your model and issue to begin the review process.
