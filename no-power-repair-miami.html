No Power Repair FAQs

Answers to common questions about no power repair services.

Can you fix a device that will not turn on?

We diagnose and repair many no-power issues depending on the cause of the failure. The issue may be related to battery failure, charging port damage, liquid exposure, board-level faults, or another internal problem.

Is no-power repair always possible?

Not always. Some devices may have severe board damage, liquid corrosion, unavailable parts, or repair costs that are not practical. Final repair options are confirmed after device diagnostics.

Should I keep trying to charge the device?

If the device has liquid exposure, overheating, swelling, burning smell, or visible damage, stop charging it and submit a repair request as soon as possible.

What details should I include in the repair request?

Include the device model, what happened before it stopped turning on, charging behavior, liquid exposure history, drop history, and any photos or videos that show the issue.

Do you offer a warranty on no power repairs?

Yes. Eligible no-power repairs include warranty coverage. Warranty terms may vary depending on the repair performed and replacement parts used.

Can liquid damage cause a device to stop powering on?

Yes. Liquid exposure can damage power circuits, batteries, charging systems, connectors, and motherboard components. Immediate inspection is recommended if liquid exposure occurred before the device stopped powering on.
